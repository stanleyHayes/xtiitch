## 1. Introduction

### What Xtiitch is

Xtiitch is a fashion platform built for Ghana. It gives any fashion business — from a one-person seamstress selling on Instagram to an established boutique — three things at once:

- **A real, branded online store** that customers can browse and order from.
- **A simple business dashboard** to run orders, fittings, payments, and the storefront from one place.
- **A clear answer to "where is my cloth?"** — a tracking link that shows every customer exactly where their garment has reached.

Xtiitch sells both **ready-made** pieces (pick a size and buy) and **bespoke / made-to-measure** work (measurements taken, a deposit paid, the balance settled later) — often from the same shop at the same time.

### The three surfaces (and who uses each)

| Surface | Who it is for | Where you find it |
|---|---|---|
| **Storefront** | Shoppers and customers | `store.xtiitch.com` and each shop's own address, `your-shop.xtiitch.com` |
| **Business dashboard** | Shop owners, admins, and staff | `business.xtiitch.com` |
| **Admin console** | Xtiitch platform operators | the operator sign-in page |

### The money principle: Xtiitch never holds your funds

This is the single rule that shapes everything about payments:

- Every customer payment settles **directly to the business's own settlement account** through **Paystack** (Ghana's payment processor). Xtiitch runs no wallet and no escrow.
- Xtiitch takes only a **small, transparent commission**, split off automatically as the money flows through the platform. There is never anything to chase or invoice.
- **Card details never touch Xtiitch** — Paystack handles all card and mobile-money collection on its own secure page.
- Money taken **outside** Xtiitch (cash in the shop, or mobile money sent straight to the business) carries **no fee at all** — it never passed through the platform, so it stays 100% with the business.

Customers pay in **Ghana Cedis (GHS)** by **mobile money** or **card** (including international cards).

## 2. For shoppers (customers)

You do **not** need an account to browse or even to place an order. A light account is only created at the last step when you order, and you can optionally sign in to keep your order history in one place.

### 2.1 Discovering shops and designs

**On the marketplace (`store.xtiitch.com`):**

1. The home page is the **Marketplace**. You will see a headline inviting you to *"Browse studios and their designs, or describe what you want and let AI find it."*
2. Use the **AI search** box — type what you want in plain words, for example *"red kente dress for a wedding under 800"* — and press **Search**. Xtiitch reads your description (colour, occasion, budget) and looks across **every** shop at once.
3. Below the search, **Featured** cards show sponsored placements from verified shops.
4. Use the browse bar to switch between the **studios** and **designs** tabs, search by keyword (**Search studios** / **Search designs**), and sort by **Most designs**, **Name (A–Z)**, or (for designs) **Price: low → high** / **Price: high → low**.
5. There is a **Track an order** button on the home page so you can jump straight to order tracking.

**What you'll see:** AI search is free to try. Signed out, you get a limited number of free searches a month; the meter reads *"X of 25 free searches left"*. Signing in with your phone or email unlocks **25 free AI searches a month**.

### 2.2 Browsing a shop and opening a design

1. Open any shop to reach its **storefront** — its own branded page with a **Verified Xtiitch store** badge, a piece count, and its catalogue.
2. Use **Search pieces** to find a design inside that shop, or **Shop by collection** to browse designs grouped by theme.
3. Tap a design to open its **design page**, which shows an image gallery, description, and — depending on the piece — either the **price by size** or the **bespoke deposit**.

**What you'll see on a design page:** a "How ordering works" guide (**Choose the piece → Select a fit route → Pay or reserve → Track production**) and a **No account needed to browse** note. Ready-made pieces show a **Sizes & prices** list (each size band, its price, and any size-chart measurements). Bespoke pieces show the deposit instead.

### 2.3 Choosing a size and seeing the price

- For a **ready-made** design, open the **Order a listed size** panel, pick your **Size** from the dropdown (each option shows its price, e.g. *"Large · GHS 450"*), and the price is set. Ready-made orders are paid **in full** at checkout.
- For a **bespoke** design, the headline figure is the **deposit** (see below), because the full price can't be known until measurements and details are agreed.

### 2.4 Two ways to buy from one cart

Every design page has two panels side by side:

**A) Order a listed size** (ready-made / made-to-wear)
1. Choose your **Size**.
2. Fill in **Full name**, **Phone**, and **Email**.
3. Optionally add a **Promo code** or **Referral code** under **Rewards & codes** (these are checked before payment and reduce the amount charged when they qualify).
4. Pick a **Payment method** — **Mobile money** or **Card**.
5. Press **Pay now** to go straight to secure Paystack checkout, **or** press **Add to cart** to keep shopping.

**B) Request a bespoke fit** (made-to-measure)
This panel offers up to three routes, depending on what the shop has switched on:

- **Self-measure** — enter the shop's measurement fields yourself (*"Add at least one measurement"*), then **Pay bespoke deposit**. This is the only bespoke route that can also be **added to cart** (**Add deposit to cart**).
- **Home visit** — pick an open slot on the shop's calendar, add the **Visit address**, then **Pay visit deposit**; the tailor measures you at the visit. *(See 2.7.)*
- **Come to the shop** — **Reserve shop measurement**. This route takes **no online payment** — you visit the shop to be measured and arrange money directly with the business.

**The one cart, combined checkout:** You can put **both** a ready-made piece *and* a self-measure bespoke deposit into the **same cart** and pay for them in **one** Paystack transaction. Each item becomes its own order, and the single payment confirms them all.

### 2.5 Checkout (contact details, pickup vs delivery, one payment)

1. From your **cart**, review the items and total, then press **Proceed to checkout**.
2. **Choose how you'll receive it** (shown only when your cart has a ready-made piece and the shop offers delivery):
   - **Pick up from the studio (free)**, or
   - **Deliver to me** — then choose a **Delivery area** (each zone shows its fee), enter the **Delivery address**, and optionally a **GPS location** (a Ghana Post GPS code like *GA-123-4567*, or a Google Maps link). The delivery fee is added to your total.
3. Enter **Full name**, **Email**, and (optionally) **Phone**.
4. Press **Pay [amount] with Paystack**. You are taken to Paystack's secure page to pay by mobile money or card.

**What you'll see:** If your cart contains a bespoke item, a note reminds you *"Bespoke pieces are charged as deposits in this cart"* — the shop confirms the remaining balance after reviewing your measurements. Delivery can only be chosen when the cart includes a ready-made piece; a lone bespoke deposit is pickup/arrangement only.

### 2.6 Paying a bespoke deposit

- A bespoke order is confirmed with a **deposit** rather than the full price. The deposit **defaults to GHS 100**, can be set higher by the shop (store-wide or for one design), and **never goes below GHS 100**.
- The deposit **counts toward** the final price.
- After you pay, the shop settles the design, any changes, and the final price with you (Xtiitch encourages this over WhatsApp). The **balance** is then collected the shop's way — another Xtiitch payment link, or cash / direct mobile money.
- **Exception:** if you choose **Come to the shop** to be measured, **no deposit** is taken online; everything is arranged directly with the business.

### 2.7 Booking a home visit from the calendar

1. On a bespoke design that offers it, open the **Home visit** route.
2. Under **Choose visit day**, a month calendar shows which days have open slots (marked **Available**; greyed days are **Unavailable / fully booked**). Use the arrows to move between months.
3. Tap an available day, then pick a time slot; the selected time is shown as *"Selected: …"*.
4. Enter the **Visit address** (house number, street, area, landmark), your contact details, and a payment method.
5. Press **Pay visit deposit**. The deposit confirms the slot.

**What you'll see:** Because a booked visit is secured by a deposit, you cannot change or cancel it with a button tap — moving or cancelling is arranged by contacting the shop, which frees the slot for someone else.

### 2.8 Tracking an order

Tracking needs **no login**.

1. Use the **Track an order** page, or open the tracking link the shop sent you. Paste your **Order ID or tracking link** and press **Track order**. (Full tracking URLs work too.)
2. The status page shows a big **red / yellow / green** signal with a word and a percentage complete:
   - **Red — Order received** (work not started)
   - **Yellow — Being made** (in progress)
   - **Green — Ready** (ready for collection, on its way, or delivered)
3. Below that, every production **stage** is listed with the current stage marked **Now**, and a **Handover** panel shows pickup or delivery details once the shop adds them.

**What you'll see:** The colour is the headline and the stage name sits beneath it, so anyone can understand it at a glance. Refresh the link any time for the latest status — updates come from the shop.

### 2.9 Signing in with a WhatsApp (or email) code

Signing in is optional; it keeps your orders together and unlocks more AI searches.

1. Go to **Your account**.
2. Choose a channel: **WhatsApp** or **Email**. *(If WhatsApp codes aren't switched on, the WhatsApp tab shows "Soon" and email is used instead.)*
3. Enter your **Phone number** or **Email address** and press **Send my code**. No password is needed.
4. Enter the **6-digit code** you receive and press **Verify & sign in**. You can **Resend code / change number** if needed.

**What you'll see once signed in:** an account hub with your **Profile** (edit **Your name** and **Email**; your verified phone/email is your login and can't be changed here), **Your orders** (each with its status and a **Track** button), and a **Search with AI** shortcut. There's a **Sign out** button at the top.

## 3. For business owners

Your business dashboard (`business.xtiitch.com`) is your studio's command centre — every order, fitting, payment, and storefront update lives here. This section walks through setting up and running your shop.

> Tip: the dashboard has a built-in **How to use Xtiitch** guide (with a "Listen" option that reads it aloud) and a "?" help button on each page.

### 3.1 Signing up (create your store)

Signup is one form revealed as three quick steps:

1. **Your store** — enter your **Business name** and choose a **Store handle**. Your storefront goes live at **your-handle.xtiitch.com**. The handle is checked as you type (*"✓ … is available"* / *"already taken"* / *"reserved"*); use lowercase letters, numbers, and dashes.
2. **Your account** — enter **Your name**, **Email**, and a **Password** (at least 8 characters, entered twice). Then enter your **WhatsApp number** and press **Send code**; enter the **WhatsApp code** you receive to confirm the number. (You can **Resend code** if needed.)
3. **Choose a plan** — pick a plan; each shows its **% commission on sales** and monthly price. Press **Create store**.

**What happens next:** If you chose a **paid** plan, you go to billing setup (see 3.3). On the **Free** plan you land straight in your dashboard, ready to sell online at no monthly cost. You can upgrade any time.

### 3.2 Business verification (your Ghana Card)

Before a shop can take online payments, Xtiitch verifies the owner with a **Ghana Card** — this is required to activate a paid plan and to receive customer money. You can submit it during billing setup, or any time from the **Business verification** panel in **Settings**.

1. In the **Verify your business** panel, enter your **Ghana Card number** (format **GHA-123456789-0**).
2. **Upload a clear photo of the front of your Ghana Card** (required).
3. Save and submit.

**What you'll see:** a status chip that reads **Not verified**, **In review**, **Verified**, or **Rejected**. Once submitted, your card is on file and **in review** (pending); when an operator approves it, it shows **verified**. If it's rejected, resubmit with a correct number and clear photo. A card already on file (pending or verified) is not asked for again.

### 3.3 Setting up Paystack billing (at signup or upgrade)

Paid plans are billed through **Paystack**.

1. On the **Set up billing** screen you'll see the plan and its price (billed **quarterly** or **yearly**).
2. Provide your Ghana Card if it isn't already on file (see 3.2).
3. Press **Continue to payment** (or **Save & continue to payment**). You are redirected to **Paystack checkout**, where you **pay the first period now** by **mobile money or card**, then you're returned to your dashboard with the plan active.
4. If you paid by **card**, renewals are charged automatically each period. If you paid by **mobile money**, we can't auto-charge it, so we'll send you a one-tap reminder to pay each renewal.
5. You can **Skip for now — I'll do this later** and finish from the dashboard.
6. If you entered a promo code that makes the first period free, the plan activates immediately with no payment page.

Billing (paying Xtiitch for your plan) is **separate** from payouts (customers paying **you**). Setting up billing does **not** set up how you get paid — do that next.

### 3.3a Set up payouts — how you get paid (required)

**Until you set up payouts, customers can't check out from your store** — the storefront shows *"We couldn't start that payment"*, because there's nowhere to send your money. This is a one-time, ~30-second step.

1. Go to **Settings → Payout details** (or tap **Set up payouts** on the Overview banner / your setup checklist).
2. Choose your **mobile money network** — **MTN MoMo**, **Telecel (Vodafone) Cash**, or **AT (AirtelTigo) Money**.
3. Enter your **mobile money number** (the one that receives the money — it must be a real number registered to you).
4. Press **Save payout details**.

That's it. From then on, when a customer pays, **your share goes straight to that MoMo number** and Xtiitch keeps only its commission — Xtiitch never holds your money. You never log in to Paystack; you only enter your MoMo number here. The "Payouts set up" step on your Overview checklist turns green once it's done.

> Your first settlement may take a little longer while the payment provider confirms the account, but customers can pay you right away.

### 3.4 Getting around the dashboard

A left rail lists your workspaces (grouped as **Overview**, **Operations**, **Storefront**, **Setup**, and **Command**). The top bar shows your live store link (**{your-handle}.xtiitch.com**), a notifications bell, a help button, a theme (light/dark) toggle, and your profile menu with **Sign out**.

The workspaces are:

- **Overview** — your studio's pulse: today's live orders, tracked money, upcoming fittings, pending handovers, and unread messages, each linking to its full page.
- **Tasks** — the staff shift queue (see section 4).
- **Reports** — revenue and order-volume trends over a time range.
- **Orders** — your production board (3.6).
- **Money** — tracked income and takings (3.7).
- **Visits** — your fitting queue / appointment book (3.8).
- **Handovers** — pickup and delivery (3.9).
- **Catalogue** — the designs on your storefront (3.5).
- **Promotions** — discount codes (3.10).
- **Measurements** — the measurement fields your studio uses (3.5).
- **Availability** — the hours customers can book visits (3.8).
- **Settings** — store switches, branding, and features (3.11).
- **Team** — staff and access roles (section 4).
- **Messages** — the outbox of updates sent to customers.

### 3.5 The Design Studio (Catalogue, sizes & measurements)

The **Catalogue** page is your Design Studio. It opens with a two-column setup panel — **Collections** on one side, **Size bands** on the other — plus your designs.

**Set up your collections and size bands first:**

1. **Create a collection** — give it a name and an optional **theme** (a short description that gives it a feel and story, such as a bridal line or a festive range). Collections are ordered automatically.
2. **Create a size band** — the fit options customers choose from (e.g. Small, Medium, Large, or Custom). Give it a **label** and order.
3. **Build the size chart** for a band by adding rows of **measurement / value / unit**. Use the **quick-add chips for common measurements** (chest, waist, hip, length, and the like) to drop in a standard row in one tap, then fill in the value. These measurements appear on the design page so customers can check their fit.

**Add your designs:**

1. **Add a design** with a **title**, a **description**, and **images** — drag and drop into the image dropzone, or choose files to upload (images upload directly to secure storage, never through Xtiitch's servers).
2. Set pricing in the **per-size-band pricing grid**: for a **made-to-wear** piece, enter a price for each size band; the customer picks a size and pays that price in full at checkout.
3. For a **bespoke** piece, set a **deposit** instead of a full price. The deposit **defaults to GHS 100**, can be set higher (store-wide or for one design), and **never goes below GHS 100**. It counts toward the final price, which you agree with the customer afterward.
4. **Publish** a piece to make it live; **retire** it to take it down (retired items stay safely in your dashboard and can be **restored** any time; deleting is a separate, deliberate action).
5. Use the **copy-link** button on a design or collection to grab its **shareable link** for Instagram, Facebook, or WhatsApp — post a single design or a whole collection and send customers straight to it.

**Measurement fields:** On the **Measurements** page, define the fields your studio takes (for example chest, waist, length). These fields then appear when a customer self-measures at checkout and when you record measurements on an order.

### 3.6 Managing Orders

The **Orders** page holds everything — online orders, walk-ins, and custom pieces. Each order moves through clear stages from **awaiting payment** to **fulfilled**, and the customer follows the same progress on their red/yellow/green tracking link.

**Two views — switch with the toggle:**

- **Table** — a searchable list. Type in the **search** box to find an order, and use the channel filter buttons **All / Online / Walk-in** to focus on one source. Click a row to open the order's detail drawer.
- **Board** — a kanban board with a column per production stage; see everything grouped by where it is.

**Working an order:**

1. **Open an order** (row in Table, or card on the Board) to see the customer, the piece, the measurements, the payment/balance, and the agreed price. There's a button to **copy the tracking link** to send the customer.
2. **Advance the stage** as work progresses — the customer's tracking view updates automatically. You define your own stages per flow (ready-made vs bespoke); each stage is tied to one of the three customer colours (red / yellow / green).
3. For a bespoke order, **set the agreed total** once the price is settled and **collect the balance** with a payment link when the piece is ready.

**Recording a walk-in or in-person bespoke order:**

1. Open the **add order** panel (the walk-in panel).
2. Choose the type: **ready-made** (pick a **size band**, which sets the price) or **bespoke** (record the customer's **measurements** now and price it later).
3. Enter the customer's details and save. It now lives alongside your online orders and moves through the same stages.

### 3.7 Money

The **Money** desk is a clear record of what you've earned (Xtiitch never holds your funds). A row of summary tiles shows **Through platform** (Paystack income), **Commission** (Xtiitch's share of platform payments), **Manual takings** (what you logged yourself), **Offline commission due**, and **Net income**.

1. **Review payments** that came through checkout (Paystack) alongside takings you log yourself, in a paginated list.
2. **Log a taking** — press the log button to record a **cash** or **direct mobile-money** sale so your offline income is counted too. These offline takings are **fee-free** — commission applies only to payments that pass through Paystack, never to money taken outside the platform.
3. **Check commission and net income** to understand your true earnings. Delivery fees you collect are part of the picture.

### 3.8 Availability & the visit queue

**Availability** sets the visit hours customers can book against. You add one or more **windows**, and each window has a **recurrence**:

1. Choose the recurrence: **Every day**, **Weekly**, **Monthly**, or **Ongoing**.
   - **Weekly** shows a **weekday** picker (e.g. every Tuesday).
   - **Monthly** shows a **day-of-month** picker (e.g. the 1st).
2. Set the window's **start** and **end** times, and the **slot length** (in minutes) — Xtiitch splits the window into bookable slots.
3. Add more windows for other days, and remove or edit any window as busy seasons and holidays change. Only these times appear on customers' home-visit calendars.

**Visits** is your fitting queue — today's and upcoming fittings, both booked-online and walk-in. Open a visit to see the customer and the order it relates to; you can **reschedule** or **cancel** a booking, and mark visits attended so the schedule stays accurate.

### 3.9 Handovers (pickup & delivery)

The **Handovers** desk covers the final step — getting the finished garment to the customer. It is segmented into **Delivery** and **Pickup**, with a **status filter** so you can focus on what's outstanding. Finished (fulfilled) orders that don't yet have an open handover are surfaced here so nothing slips.

1. Pick **Delivery** or **Pickup** and use the status filter to see what's waiting.
2. **Arrange a handover** — confirm the method and the customer's details (recipient name, phone, address, courier, and a note).
3. **Advance** it to **dispatched** and then **completed** (or **cancel** it). The status shows on the customer's tracking page as **Arranged → On the way → Completed**.

### 3.10 Promotions

**Promotions** lets you create discount codes (a paid-plan feature).

1. **Create a code** and choose a **percentage** or **fixed** discount.
2. **Set limits** — how many times it can be used, when it expires, and a minimum spend.
3. **Scope** it to your whole store, a collection, or a single design.
4. Share the code with customers; deactivate it any time. A code is only finally redeemed after a successful payment.

### 3.11 Store settings, customization, delivery zones & verification

On **Settings** you control how your storefront looks and behaves:

- **Feature switches** — turn on only what fits how you work: **bespoke**, **measurements**, **customisation**, **collections**, **delivery**, and **dispatch**. A ready-to-wear boutique can switch measurements and customisation off; a bespoke house switches them on. A single store can run both.
- **Branding (plan-gated):** your **brand accent colour** (Starter and up), and on higher plans a **custom logo**, **custom hero banner**, and **storefront layout variant** (standard / spotlight / minimal). Options your plan doesn't include are shown locked with an upgrade prompt.
- **Delivery zones:** in the delivery-zones panel, add each area you deliver to and its **fee**. These are exactly the zones and prices customers choose from at checkout.
- **Business verification:** the Ghana Card panel (see 3.2), showing your current status chip.

Some options depend on your plan — upgrade to unlock more.

### 3.12 Team, billing & security

- **Team** — invite members by name and email and choose a role (owner / admin / staff). Owners and admins manage everything; staff get the day-to-day workspaces. You can change or remove access any time, and transfer ownership to another admin with a confirmation step. (See section 4.)
- **Billing / plans** — view all plans and your current subscription, and switch plan or billing cycle (**monthly / quarterly / yearly**) yourself from the dashboard.
- **Security** — turn on two-step verification and change your password from the **Security** page. (See section 6.)

## 4. For staff

Xtiitch supports three team roles. Owners and admins add staff from the **Team** page and choose each person's role.

| Role | What they can do |
|---|---|
| **Owner** | Full control of everything, including money, billing, settings, team, and business verification. Ownership can be transferred to another admin with an explicit confirmation step. |
| **Admin** | Manages everything day-to-day, alongside the owner. |
| **Staff** | The day-to-day production workspaces — the shift queue and order/fitting/handover work — **without** the management-only numbers and sensitive areas like money and billing. |

**What staff see:** Staff sign in to the same dashboard but land on a **Tasks** ("your shift queue") view — the orders to move forward, fittings to prepare, and handovers to complete for their shift. They advance orders through production stages, mark fittings attended, and complete handovers, so the rest of the team sees progress. Sensitive areas (money totals, billing, plan changes, team management, verification) are limited to owners and admins by their role.

Owners and admins can remove or change a team member's access at any time as the team changes.

## 5. For platform admins / operators

The **admin console** (`admin.xtiitch.com`) is the operator control room. It is a restricted, separate sign-in from shops and shoppers. This section covers the tasks operators do most; the console has more tools than are listed here.

### 5.1 Signing in

1. On **Sign in to Admin**, enter your **Operator email** and **Password**, then press **Open console**.
2. Operator accounts are managed centrally — there is no self-service sign-up. If you forget your password, press **Forgot password?**; you'll be told to ask the platform owner to reset it.

**What you'll see:** the console opens on **Overview** ("Everything at a glance" — the platform pulse). The left rail groups sections under **Growth**, **Access**, **Operations**, and **Command**. A "?" icon on each section title opens a contextual guide. The top bar has notifications, a theme toggle, and a **Profile and settings** avatar menu.

### 5.2 Reviewing Ghana Card verifications (approve / reject / hold)

This is the KYC gate that lets a business take money. Open **Verification** (the **Payment verification queue**).

1. Each business waiting for a decision appears as a case card showing the business name, `handle.xtiitch.com`, the owner's name and email, plus a **risk chip** and **status chip**. Press **Review case** to expand it.
2. Inspect the **Ghana Card** (click the card image to open it full size) and its **Card number**, plus the **Documents**, **Checks**, and **Evidence** panels.
3. Write a reason in the **Operator note** field (*"Record why this case is approved, rejected, or held."*).
4. Decide:
   - **Approve** — the business becomes **verified** and can take online payments.
   - **Reject** — sends it back; the owner must resubmit a correct card number (format `GHA-123456789-0`) and a clear photo.
   - **Hold** — keeps it pending while you follow up.

**What you'll see:** verification statuses are **pending / verified / rejected**. Only approve when identity, settlement account, and notes are clean.

### 5.3 Managing businesses & their status

Open **Businesses** ("Tenant control").

1. Use **Search business** and the **Status** filter (**All statuses / Unverified / Pending / Verified / Rejected / Suspended**), and switch between **List view** and **Card view**.
2. The table shows **Business | GMV | Commission | Last active | Actions**.
3. From the **Business actions** menu (or the row buttons) choose **Inspect**, or **Suspend** / **Reactivate** a tenant.
4. **Inspect** opens a side drawer with the owner, Paystack **Subaccount** reference, orders, GMV, commission, and last-active — plus **Admin-safe actions**: enter a **Suspension reason** and **Suspend business** (or **Reactivate business**), **Review payments**, **Open audit trail**, or **View public storefront**.

Suspending a business never touches customer data.

### 5.4 Managing plans & packages

Open **Subscriptions**, then the **Package controls** area. Press **New package** to open **Create package**.

- **Identity:** **Code** (e.g. `pro-plus`) and **Name**.
- **Pricing & limits:** **Monthly fee** and **Yearly fee** (in GHS), **Commission** in **bps** (basis points — 100 bps = 1%), and **Design limit** (leave blank for unlimited).
- **Package benefits** — tick the storefront features this plan unlocks: **Storefront accent colour**, **Custom storefront logo**, **Custom hero banner image**, **Storefront layout variants**, **Design waiting lists**, and **Online ordering & checkout**. Businesses on a plan without a benefit see it locked with an upgrade prompt.

Existing plans can be edited or **archived** (with an **Archive reason**) rather than deleted, so businesses keep their history. Each plan card shows **Monthly fee / Yearly fee / Commission (as %) / Design limit / Monthly recurring** tiles.

### 5.5 Subscriptions & recurring billing

Still under **Subscriptions**, operators see revenue tiles (**Estimated base MRR**, **Commission revenue**, **Lifecycle attention**, **Over design limit**, **Free upgrade candidates**) and a **Billing operations** panel:

- **Run sweep** — fail overdue invoices and cancel subscriptions whose grace window has expired.
- **Run charges** — charge due recurring subscriptions through saved Paystack authorizations.

Per business, **Manage billing** lets you set the **Status** (Active / Trialing / Past due / Grace period / Cancel at period end / Canceled) and **Mode** (Manual / Payment link / Recurring), manage the **Recurring authorization** (**Create auth** opens a Paystack checkout where the business pays the current period and — if they pay by card — leaves a reusable authorization for future renewals; **Verify auth** confirms that payment and books the paid period), and control invoices — **Issue invoice**, **Mark paid**, or **Mark failed** (statuses: Issued / Paid / Failed / Void). Aside from the first-period checkout that **Create auth** starts, no funds move until a charge or invoice action explicitly triggers it.

### 5.6 Launch-readiness checks

Open **Readiness** ("Launch readiness" — the go-live checklist), also reachable from the profile menu.

- Top tiles show a **Readiness score** (gates ready / total), **Blocked gates**, **Watch gates**, and the **Environment**.
- A **Production gate posture** banner summarises **ready / watch / blocked** counts.
- Each gate is a card (credentials, provider transports, legal review, quality scans, owner-controlled config) with a status chip and a button that jumps to the section that clears it. Blocked gates are highlighted in red and must be cleared before public launch.

### 5.7 Platform settings (including the brand logo)

Open **Settings** ("Profile, platform settings, and notifications").

- **Profile settings** — your **Display name** and **Email**; **Save profile**.
- **Platform settings** (owner / manage-settings only) — **Platform name**, **Support email**, **Verification SLA hours**, **Payout review threshold**, and the **Platform logo**: **Upload logo** (PNG/JPEG/SVG/WebP) to rebrand every surface (admin, marketing, dashboard, storefronts), or tick **Remove logo** to fall back to the built-in Xtiitch mark. There is also a **Maintenance mode** toggle. Press **Save platform settings**.
- **Marketing launch flags** — turn public marketing-site features on/off instantly (no redeploy): **Browse the store**, **Discover menu & featured businesses**, **Create your store button**, **Pricing plan buttons**.
- **Notification settings** — timezone, SMS phone, digest time, and per-area alert toggles.

### 5.8 Other operator tools (brief)

- **Money rails** — watch Paystack **Webhook ledger** (with **Replay** and payment-reversal actions) and place / release **settlement holds**, without touching tenant funds.
- **Promotions**, **Ads** (sponsored placements), **Affiliates**, **Referrals** — platform-wide growth campaigns with their own create forms, scopes, and lifecycle states.

## 6. Sign-in & security for everyone

Xtiitch keeps three separate kinds of account: **shoppers**, **business users** (owner/admin/staff), and **platform operators**. Each signs in on its own surface.

### 6.1 Shoppers

- Sign in on **Your account** with a **one-time code** sent to your **WhatsApp** or **Email** — no password. (See 2.9.)

### 6.2 Business users

You can sign in to the business dashboard three ways:

- **Password** — enter your **Store handle**, **Email**, and **Password**, then **Sign in**.
- **WhatsApp code** — switch to the **WhatsApp** method, enter your **Store handle** and **WhatsApp number**, press **Send code**, then enter the code and **Verify and continue**. You can **Resend code**.
- **Two-step verification (if enabled)** — after your password or WhatsApp code, you are asked to **Enter your code** from your authenticator app (or a backup code).

**Turning on two-step verification (2FA):** From **Security**:
1. Press **Set up two-step verification**.
2. Add the account to an authenticator app (**Google Authenticator**, **Authy**, etc.) by scanning the setup link or entering the key by hand.
3. Enter the **6-digit code** it shows and press **Turn on two-step**.
4. **Save your backup codes** — each works once if you lose your phone; they are shown only once.

To turn it off, enter a current authenticator or backup code and press **Disable two-step**. The **Security** page also lets you **Change password** (enter your current password, then a new one, at least 8 characters).

### 6.3 Platform operators

- Operators sign in on the admin console's own login page. *(See section 5.)*

### 6.4 If you're locked out (business password reset)

1. On the sign-in page press **Forgot password?**
2. Enter your account **Email** and press **Send reset code**. (For privacy, Xtiitch always says a code is on its way whether or not the email is registered.)
3. Enter the **6-digit code** (it expires in 15 minutes) and choose a **New password** (at least 8 characters), then **Set new password**.
4. Sign in with your new password.

## 7. Payments & money explained

### 7.1 Plans & pricing

Xtiitch has four plans. You can start free and upgrade any time; every plan — including Free — lets customers **order and pay online**. What changes between plans is how much room and branding you get, and the **share of each online sale** Xtiitch takes.

| Plan | Monthly price | What it adds |
|---|---|---|
| **Free — Get Online** | GHS 0 | Your own store link, a branded catalogue storefront, card & mobile-money checkout, up to about **10 designs**, walk-in / manual order logging, order tracking. |
| **Starter — Start Selling** | GHS 49 | Everything in Free, up to about **50 designs**, your **storefront accent colour**, full project tracking, the money tracker, **delivery zones & fees**, two-step sign-in. |
| **Growth — Run the Business** *(Most popular)* | GHS 99 | Everything in Starter, **unlimited designs & collections**, **custom logo / hero banner / layout variants**, **design waiting lists**, **discount codes & promotions**, deeper analytics, up to **2 staff logins**, priority support. |
| **Studio — Scale Up** | GHS 199 | Everything in Growth, up to **10 staff logins** with roles & permissions, multiple store locations, advanced reports, priority placement, dedicated onboarding. |

**Billing cycles:** pay **monthly**, pay **quarterly** (save about 20%), or pay **yearly** (get about 3 months free). You can switch your plan or billing cycle yourself from the dashboard at any time.

*Plans and their exact prices, design limits, and features are configured by Xtiitch operators and can change; the table above reflects the published plans.*

### 7.2 Commission (and why offline sales are free)

- Xtiitch takes its **small percentage on every payment that passes through the platform** — every checkout and every payment link, whether for a ready-made piece, a custom order, or a booking deposit. It is split off automatically as the money flows, so there is never anything to chase.
- **The commission gets smaller as you move up a plan** (Free → Starter → Growth → Studio) — so the more you sell online, the less you pay per sale.
- **Money taken outside Xtiitch is fee-free.** Cash in the shop, or mobile money a customer sends you directly, carries **no commission at all** — it never passed through the platform. You can still log it in your money tracker for a complete record; Xtiitch takes nothing from what it did not handle.

### 7.3 Paystack, delivery fees, and how money settles

- When a business joins, Xtiitch sets up a **Paystack subaccount** behind the scenes. To the business it feels like one smooth Xtiitch signup; you never have to navigate Paystack yourself.
- From then on, customer payments settle **directly to the business's own settlement account / mobile money**. Xtiitch's commission sits **on top of Paystack's own transaction fee (1.95%)** — that 1.95% goes to the payment processor, not to Xtiitch.
- **Delivery fees** are set by the business per **delivery zone**. When a customer chooses delivery, the zone's fee is added to the Paystack charge.

### 7.4 Deposits and balances (bespoke)

- Ready-made orders are **paid in full** at checkout (the price is known).
- Bespoke orders are confirmed with a **deposit** (defaults to GHS 100, never lower), which counts toward the final price. The **balance** is agreed and collected afterward — by another Xtiitch payment link (commission applies) or cash / direct mobile money (fee-free). Choosing **Come to the shop** to be measured takes no deposit.

### 7.5 Subscription billing & renewal

- Choosing a paid plan starts a **recurring Paystack authorization**: after signup (or when you upgrade), you set up billing and are redirected to Paystack to authorize the monthly/quarterly/yearly charge. You can manage or cancel it any time.
- A **paid plan usually works out cheaper overall** than Free once you're selling steadily online, because the lower per-sale commission more than covers the small subscription fee.

### 7.6 Cancellations & refunds

Because payments settle directly to the business and Xtiitch holds no funds, **refund and cancellation requests start with the business**. Xtiitch records the request, the payment status, and any provider-confirmed reversal, so the customer, the business, and the operator can all see the same trail.

## 8. FAQ & troubleshooting

**I can't sign in to my shop dashboard.**
Check you're using your **store handle**, the **owner email**, and the right **password**. If two-step verification is on, you also need a code from your authenticator app (or a backup code). Forgotten your password? Use **Forgot password?** to get a 6-digit email reset code. You can also sign in with a **WhatsApp** code using your store handle and WhatsApp number.

**A customer's order isn't showing in my dashboard.**
Orders appear once payment is confirmed by Paystack. A ready-made order sits in **awaiting payment / draft** until the customer completes checkout; a bespoke order confirms once the **deposit** is paid. Come-to-shop bespoke requests are reserved without an online payment, so log/confirm them from the dashboard.

**The payment didn't start / checkout wouldn't open.**
The most common cause is that the shop hasn't finished **payment verification** yet — a store must be verified (with settlement details in its own name) before it can take online orders or deposits. Other checkout messages ask you to re-check the selected size and contact details, or to remove a promo code that doesn't qualify for that order. Wait a moment and try again if it was a temporary glitch.

**My verification is still "pending" — can I take payments?**
A submitted Ghana Card sits **under review**. You can finish setting up billing, but customers can't complete online payments until the business is **verified/approved**. If it's rejected, you'll be asked to resubmit with a correct card number (format `GHA-123456789-0`) and a clear photo of the card front.

**A shopper says they can't find their order status.**
No login is needed — send them the **tracking link**, or point them to the **Track an order** page where they paste the **Order ID** or the link. The page only opens a tracking view; it never exposes account or payment details.

**I didn't get my one-time code (WhatsApp or email).**
Request it again (**Resend code**). WhatsApp codes only work when WhatsApp messaging is switched on for the platform; if the WhatsApp option shows **"Soon"**, use **Email** instead. Codes expire, so use the latest one.

**A promo/referral code isn't applying.**
Codes are validated at checkout, just before Paystack opens. If a code doesn't qualify (wrong scope, expired, minimum spend not met, usage cap reached), you'll be told to check it, remove it, or try a different reward — the order can still go through without a code.

**Why is my delivery greyed out at checkout?**
Delivery is offered only when your cart includes a **ready-made** piece and the shop has set up delivery zones. A cart with only a bespoke deposit is pickup / arrange-with-shop.

**Does Xtiitch hold my money?**
No. Customer payments settle **directly to the business** through Paystack. Xtiitch takes only a small commission as the money flows and never holds funds — which is also why refunds start with the business.

**Are card details safe?**
Yes. Paystack collects all card and mobile-money details on its own secure page. Card details never touch Xtiitch, and each business's data is sealed off from every other business.
