# Marketing Website Plan

The marketing website is part of Xtiitch v1. It is the public acquisition surface for fashion businesses and the education surface for customers who want to understand the product before trusting it.

## Primary Audience

- Ghanaian tailors and seamstresses.
- Bespoke and made-to-measure fashion houses.
- Ready-to-wear boutiques.
- Instagram and WhatsApp sellers who need a real store.
- Small teams that want order structure, payments, and customer trust without paying for a custom platform.

## Secondary Audience

- Customers who receive Xtiitch links from a business.
- Partners, payment reviewers, press, and future investors.
- Larger fashion brands evaluating whether Xtiitch can support their operations.

## Positioning

Xtiitch gives a fashion business a real online store, a dashboard to run orders and customers, a clean way to take payment, and a customer tracking view that answers: where is my cloth?

## Core Pages

- Home: promise, primary CTA, product preview, trust signals.
- Features: storefront, catalogue, orders, tracking, payments, bookings, money tracker, notifications.
- How it works: set up store, upload designs, share links, receive orders, take payments, move garments through stages.
- Pricing: Free, Standard, and future Growth package explanation.
- For customers: how tracking, payments, delivery/pickup, and order updates work.
- Security and trust: direct Paystack payments, no Xtiitch-held funds, tenant isolation, data protection posture.
- FAQ: deposits, custom orders, mobile money, cards, subscriptions, cancellation/refund policy once approved.
- Contact/join waitlist: lead capture for businesses before full onboarding is live.

## Key Benefits To Communicate

- Get a professional store without hiring a developer.
- Share one design link on Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp.
- Turn scattered chats into clean order records.
- Let customers track progress instead of calling repeatedly.
- Take mobile money and card payments through Paystack.
- Keep cash and direct mobile money takings visible in one tracker.
- Run bespoke and ready-made work side by side.
- Start free, then upgrade as sales grow.

## Proof And Research Cues

Use the product docs first. Supporting market context:

- Ghana has a strong online commerce and mobile money environment.
- Fashion is a high-fit category for visual discovery and social sharing.
- MSMEs often need digital tools that reduce friction rather than heavy enterprise systems.
- Digital records can later support insights, lending, and growth services with consent.

Do not overclaim market data. If a number is used in copy, cite the source in the page content or supporting notes.

## Conversion Goals

- Business joins waitlist.
- Business starts onboarding once signup exists.
- Business requests setup help.
- Customer understands and trusts an order tracking link.
- Visitor can explain Xtiitch in one sentence after the first screen.

## SEO Topics

- Fashion business software Ghana.
- Online store for tailors in Ghana.
- Tailor order tracking app.
- Mobile money payments for fashion businesses.
- Boutique storefront Ghana.
- Bespoke tailoring management app.

## Design Requirements

- Follow `docs/design/style-guide.md`.
- Use real garment and work-process imagery.
- Avoid generic SaaS illustrations.
- Make the first viewport clearly about Xtiitch and fashion businesses.
- Keep pricing plain and locally understandable.
- Use the burgundy/ink/cream palette without making the whole site monochrome.
- Mobile-first layout is mandatory.

## Compliance Requirements

- Marketing claims must match built or committed product behavior.
- Payment claims must be consistent with Paystack integration and direct-settlement posture.
- Pricing and subscription terms must be clear.
- Refund/cancellation statements must wait for approved policy.
- Privacy and terms links must exist before public launch.
